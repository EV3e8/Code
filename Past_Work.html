2025-2026

My main work in this year covered improving the original solutions to Lambert's problem, by taking the effects of other planets into consideration. Lambert's problem is defined as the issue of how to transfer between two points in space wihtin a gravitational field, in a given time. The standard solution is based on analyzing the orbits as two body gravity. However, when I was testing the solutions that were created by the normal methods for the problem, I noticed that they were not very accurate, missing by up 100,000 to 1,000,000 km. After testing the same thing, after removing the gravitaionl influence of other planets, I noticed that the algorithm wworked far better, missing by only around 10 km. This caused me to seek a way to improve the method for the model under conditions of realistic gravity. Since the problem was quite vast, I first consrained the launch and landing time and location to a realistic range. Based on recent interest in such missions, I selected transfers from the Lagrange point L1 and Mars.
